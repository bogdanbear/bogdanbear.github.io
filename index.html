About me

Thesis

First year of master research internship

Sieving Algorithms for the Shortest Vector Problem, supervised by Prof. Serge Vaudenay

During this internship, I have studied algorithms for solving the shortest vector problem in generic Euclidean lattices. I have surveyed both provable and heuristic approaches, principally the AKS, the NV-Sieve and its improvements using locality sensitive-hashing. I have also proposed an improved analysis for the case of the approximate shortest vector problem.

Diploma thesis

Factorization and DLP in a Subexponential Setting, supervised by Prof. F.L. Tiplea

During this project, I have mainly studied Gaudry's index calculus attack for hyperelliptic curves of small genus, along with an improvement proposed by Thériault. Providing an implementation requires a known cardinality of the Jacobian. To this end, I have generated my own hyperelliptic curves using a method proposed by Koblitz to construct curves with easily computable Jacobian cardinality, by means of the associated zeta function.
